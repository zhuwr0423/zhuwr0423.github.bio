Wanrong Zhu

I am a final-year PhD student at the Department of Statistics, University of Chicago. I am fortunate to be advised by Professors Rina Foygel Barber and Wei Biao Wu.

I am generally interested in statistical inference problems, with my research situated at the intersection of statistics, machine learning, and stochastic optimization. My recent work focuses on the development of methods and theoretical foundations in uncertainty quantification for online learning and stochastic optimization. Additionally, I am exploring goodness-of-fit testing and predictive inference related to selection and conditioning.
